# Course Part to CEFR Level Mapping

**Status**: CONFIRMED

This reference document defines the alignment between Professor Bill Worden's 6 Course Workbooks (Parts 2 through 7, covering Lessons 5 to 30) and the Common European Framework of Reference for Languages (CEFR) levels used in the application.

## Part-Level Default Mapping

| Course Workbook Part | Lessons Included | Primary Topic Focus | Assigned CEFR Target Level |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :---: |
| **Part 2** | Lessons 5–8 | Articles, Gender, Estar, -ER/-IR Verbs, Ir & Questions | **A1** |
| **Part 3** | Lessons 9–12 | Dates, Time, Tener Idioms, Weather, Saber vs Conocer | **A2** |
| **Part 4** | Lessons 13–16 | Stem-Changing Boot Verbs, Yo-Go Verbs, Present Progressive, DOPs | **A2** |

| **Part 7** | Lessons 27–30 | Imperfect Tense, Preterite Irregulars, Preterite vs Imperfect, Superlatives | **B2** |

## Documented Lesson-Level Exceptions & Confirmed Decisions

The following six lessons were evaluated for pedagogical difficulty relative to their Part's default level assignment and have been confirmed as follows:

1. **Lesson 16 (Direct Object Pronouns & Adverbs in -mente)**:
   - **Default Part Level**: A2 (Part 4)
   - **Confirmed Level**: **A2** (`a2.json`)
   - **Decision Rationale**: Maintained at A2 to keep initial direct object pronoun introduction (*lo/la/los/las*) alongside foundational adverbs at early stage.

2. **Lesson 20 (Double Object Pronouns)**:
   - **Default Part Level**: B1 (Part 5)
   - **Confirmed Level**: **B1** (`b1.json`)
   - **Decision Rationale**: Maintained at B1 where indirect + direct clitic combination rules (*le/les* $\rightarrow$ *se*) fit intermediate syntax progression.

3. **Lesson 21 (Reflexive Verbs & Daily Routine)**:
   - **Default Part Level**: B1 (Part 5)
   - **Confirmed Level**: **A2** (`a2.json`)
   - **Decision Rationale**: **MOVED TO A2**. Daily routine reflexives (*lavarse*, *despertarse*, *vestirse*) are standard A2 daily life communication across international CEFR frameworks.

4. **Lesson 26 (Preterite Past Tense Regular Conjugations)**:
   - **Default Part Level**: B1 (Part 6)
   - **Confirmed Level**: **A2** (`a2.json`)
   - **Decision Rationale**: **MOVED TO A2**. Regular past tense narration (*hablé*, *comí*, *viví*) is foundational A2 grammar essential for describing past events.

5. **Lesson 27 (Imperfect Tense)**:
   - **Default Part Level**: B2 (Part 7)
   - **Confirmed Level**: **B1** (`b1.json`)
   - **Decision Rationale**: **MOVED TO B1**. Descriptive past tense and habits (*hablaba*, *comía*, *era*, *iba*) form core B1 intermediate narration before advanced B2 topics.

6. **Lesson 29 (Preterite vs. Imperfect Contrast)**:
   - **Default Part Level**: B2 (Part 7)
   - **Confirmed Level**: **B1** (`b1.json`)
   - **Decision Rationale**: **MOVED TO B1**. Aspectual past contrast (*mientras* vs *de repente*, SIMBA vs WATERS) is a primary B1 milestone in Spanish language proficiency.

7. **Lessons 28 & 30 (Preterite Irregulars; Superlatives & Synthesis)**:
   - **Default Part Level**: B2 (Part 7)
   - **Confirmed Level**: **B2** (`b2.json`)
   - **Decision Rationale**: Irregular preterite stem changes (*tuv-*, *estuv-*, *hic-*, *pud-*) and absolute superlatives (*-ísimo*) remain at B2 as originally assigned.
